Savings Accounts:

Most banks offer savings accounts to help customers accumulate funds for future use. These accounts usually pay interest, allowing customers to grow their savings over time.

Checking Accounts:

Checking accounts are designed for customers to use their money for day-to-day expenses. These accounts usually come with a debit card and checks for easy access to funds.

Online Banking:

Many banks now offer online banking services that enable customers to manage their accounts from any computer or mobile device. Customers can check their balances, transfer funds, and pay bills without ever leaving home.
